Retention Policy

Last updated: May 2026

Lexoral is committed to balancing operational needs with the principle of data minimization. We retain client materials only as long as necessary to deliver our services and meet professional and legal obligations.

1. Purpose of Retention

We retain recordings, transcripts, and related files for the following legitimate purposes:

  • Service delivery and quality assurance review
  • Client support and dispute resolution
  • Professional liability protection
  • Compliance with legal or regulatory requirements

2. Default Retention Periods

Unless otherwise agreed in writing with the client:

  • Audio Recordings: Retained for up to 90 days after delivery of the final transcript.
  • Transcripts & Final Deliverables: Retained for up to 12 months after delivery.
  • Client Account & Contact Information: Retained for the duration of the business relationship plus 2 years.

3. Client Requests for Deletion

Clients may request the **permanent deletion** of any recordings, transcripts, or related materials at any time. We will process such requests promptly and confirm deletion in writing, subject only to overriding legal obligations.

4. Exceptions to Deletion

We may be required to retain certain information longer than the standard period if:

  • Retained by law, court order, or regulatory requirement
  • Necessary for the establishment, exercise, or defense of legal claims

5. Secure Handling During Retention

All retained materials remain subject to strict confidentiality protocols, encrypted storage, and restricted access. Only authorized personnel with confidentiality obligations may access client files.

6. Automatic Deletion

After the applicable retention period expires, we will securely and permanently delete client files unless a longer retention period has been requested or is legally required.
